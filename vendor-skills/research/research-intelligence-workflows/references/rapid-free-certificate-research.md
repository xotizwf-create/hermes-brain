# Rapid free-certificate research

Use this note when a user asks for very short courses/tests that issue a free named certificate.

## Validation method

Treat the request as a conjunction of constraints, and verify every one separately on the provider page:

1. **Format:** course, quiz, practice test, or certification test. Do not silently relabel a test as a course.
2. **Duration:** use the certification attempt's stated duration. If only public-question times are shown, sum them and label the result as an estimate.
3. **Actual certificate cost:** confirm that the certificate/download is free, not only the lesson or test. Watch for “free after admission,” paid downloads, subscriptions, and checkout gates.
4. **Identity:** confirm that the credential includes the participant's name and preferably has a public verification URL.
5. **Issuance condition:** distinguish automatic completion from a score/rank threshold. Never present a conditional credential as guaranteed.
6. **Topic:** verify that the questions genuinely match the requested field rather than only a broad “business” tag.

## Reporting pattern

- Put exact matches first.
- Put near matches in a separate block and state the single failed constraint (for example, 6 minutes instead of 5).
- Include direct certification links, not only home pages or search results.
- State registration, score/rank, proctoring, language, and payment conditions that affect practical completion.
- If no exact set exists, say so plainly rather than padding the list with paid or long options.

## TestDome pattern observed in July 2026

TestDome subject pages expose public questions with per-question time estimates and explicitly state that jobseekers can earn a free certificate by scoring in the top 25%. Examples verified during the session:

- Economics: two public questions, 3 + 2 minutes (estimated 5 minutes).
- Business Math: two public questions, 3 + 2 minutes (estimated 5 minutes).
- People Management: two public questions, 3 + 3 minutes (estimated 6 minutes; near match only).
- Accounting & Finance: 2 + 2 + 3 minutes (estimated 7 minutes).
- Project Management: public questions total about 11 minutes.

These are tests, not courses. The credential is conditional on reaching the top 25%, so describe it as an opportunity to earn a certificate, not as an automatic result. Recheck the live pages because question sets, timing, and certificate rules can change.
